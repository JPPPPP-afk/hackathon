  1. Ethical issues

Even though they are extremely useful, NLP algorithms still have their own flaws!

Social exclusion could be the most obvious risk related to NLP at this point. This mainly happens in the data collection process.

NLP models are mostly trained on datasets from wealthy countries, where citizens can speak standard and popular languages. For example, the US and American English. Data is extremely limited in less developed areas, where people have less exposure to advanced technologies. For example, people in rural Vietnam would be less likely to use translation tools or virtual assistant apps, potentially because they have not seen obvious conveniences/usefulness in such activities.

The same issue applies across different age groups, e.g. less data would be collected from children and elderly compared to . NLP models would therefore work less effectively with a user of ethnic minority, who have specific accents and dialects.
